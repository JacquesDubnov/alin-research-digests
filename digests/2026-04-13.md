### Quality of Life, Menopausal Symptoms, Insomnia and Depression in Peri- and Post-Menopausal Women: A Cross-Sectional Study
- **Authors:** Raczkiewicz et al.
- **Journal:** Medical Science Monitor, 2026
- **PMID:** 41955179
- **DOI:** 10.12659/MSM.951812
- **Domain:** Query 1 (HRT/MHT/menopause) & Query 2 (perimenopause/symptoms)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Directly validates ALIN's core use case — mapping the co-occurrence and severity of vasomotor symptoms, insomnia, depression, and QoL in the peri/postmenopausal window. Cross-sectional design in 287 European women provides real-world benchmarks for symptom clustering.

> Cross-sectional study of 287 peri- and postmenopausal women (ages 45–60), excluding HRT users, assessing menopausal symptoms (Greene Climacteric Scale), insomnia, depression, and quality of life. Severity of menopausal symptoms, insomnia, and depression were significantly associated with decreased quality of life across both peri- and postmenopausal groups.

### Women with PCOS Have a Later Menopausal Transition and a Lower Prevalence of Menopausal Symptoms at Age 46: A Population-Based Birth Cohort Study
- **Authors:** Lavi et al.
- **Journal:** Acta Obstetricia et Gynecologica Scandinavica, 2026
- **PMID:** 41949217
- **DOI:** 10.1111/aogs.70198
- **Domain:** Query 1 (HRT/MHT/menopause) & Query 2 (perimenopause/symptoms)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Highlights an important subgroup of users — women with PCOS — who may present differently during perimenopause. Understanding their delayed and attenuated symptom profile could inform personalised ALIN recommendations.

> Using the Northern Finland Birth Cohort 1966 (NFBC1966), women with PCOS were significantly more likely to still be in pre-/early-perimenopause at age 46 and had a lower prevalence of menopausal symptoms at that age compared to women without PCOS, suggesting a distinct menopausal transition trajectory.

### Menopause-Related Quality of Life Five Years After Salpingectomy With Delayed Oophorectomy Versus Salpingo-Oophorectomy
- **Authors:** Gootzen et al.
- **Journal:** Journal of the National Cancer Institute, 2026
- **PMID:** 41955317
- **DOI:** 10.1093/jnci/djag110
- **Domain:** Query 1 (HRT/MHT/menopause)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** BRCA1/2 carriers facing risk-reducing surgery are a distinct, high-need population. Five-year QoL data from the TUBA trial comparing salpingectomy + delayed oophorectomy vs. immediate salpingo-oophorectomy directly informs ALIN content on surgical menopause and risk management choices.

> In the nationwide TUBA preference trial, BRCA1/2 pathogenic variant carriers who chose risk-reducing salpingectomy with delayed oophorectomy reported better menopause-related quality of life at 5 years compared to those who underwent immediate salpingo-oophorectomy, supporting delayed oophorectomy as a viable strategy to postpone surgical menopause.

### Association of Menopause and Hormone Replacement Therapy with Hearing Loss
- **Authors:** Hsu et al.
- **Journal:** The Annals of Otology, Rhinology & Laryngology, 2026
- **PMID:** 41943268
- **DOI:** 10.1177/00034894261435259
- **Domain:** Query 1 (HRT/MHT/menopause)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Hearing loss is an under-discussed menopause-related concern. This epidemiological study from NHANES data may support ALIN content on the wider sensory effects of estrogen decline and the potential protective role of HRT.

> In 1,778 adult women (ages 40–69) from NHANES 2011–2012 and 2015–2016, menopause status, earlier age at menopause, and shorter reproductive lifespan were associated with greater hearing loss based on audiometry, while HRT use showed a potentially protective association — reinforcing estrogen's role in cochlear function.

### Tailoring Menopause Education to the Needs of Primary Care Clinicians: The Oregon Menopause ECHO Experience
- **Authors:** Clark et al.
- **Journal:** Menopause, 2026
- **PMID:** 41941304
- **DOI:** 10.1097/GME.0000000000002786
- **Domain:** Query 2 (perimenopause/symptoms/treatment)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Persistent gaps in clinician menopause knowledge are a structural barrier ALIN addresses by empowering patients directly. This study documents what topics PCPs need most (perimenopause, mood, hormone/non-hormone therapies, genitourinary symptoms, skeletal health) — aligning with ALIN's content domains.

> Using Project ECHO telementoring with 12 sessions for rural/under-resourced primary care clinicians, the Oregon Menopause ECHO programme identified critical educational gaps in perimenopause recognition, hormone and non-hormone therapies, genitourinary symptoms, and bone health, with participants showing significant gains in confidence and knowledge post-programme.

### Clinical Pharmacokinetics, Mass Balance and Metabolism of Fezolinetant in Postmenopausal Women
- **Authors:** Iwai et al.
- **Journal:** European Journal of Drug Metabolism and Pharmacokinetics, 2026
- **PMID:** 41954728
- **DOI:** 10.1007/s13318-026-00995-2
- **Domain:** Query 3 (non-hormonal/fezolinetant/NK3 antagonist)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Fezolinetant (Veoza) is a key non-hormonal option for vasomotor symptoms. This PK/mass balance study characterises how fezolinetant is metabolised and excreted in postmenopausal women, supporting evidence-based ALIN content on drug interactions, dosing considerations, and safety context.

> A single-dose mass balance study of ¹⁴C-fezolinetant (180 mg) in healthy postmenopausal women (n=5) characterised its pharmacokinetics, primary metabolic pathways, and routes of excretion, providing foundational data on the disposition of this NK3 receptor antagonist for treatment of moderate-to-severe vasomotor symptoms.

### Estradiol Replacement Abolishes Cardiovascular and Intracranial Changes Induced by a High-Fat Diet in Menopausal Female Rats
- **Authors:** Fernandes et al.
- **Journal:** Biomedicine & Pharmacotherapy, 2026
- **PMID:** 41966800
- **DOI:** 10.1016/j.biopha.2026.119317
- **Domain:** Query 4 (menopause + cardiovascular)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Preclinical evidence supporting estradiol's cardioprotective role, particularly in the context of obesity-associated menopause, reinforces ALIN messaging about the interplay between diet, weight, and HRT in cardiovascular health.

> In ovariectomised (OVX) rats fed a high-fat diet, subcutaneous β-estradiol replacement for 10 days abolished elevated blood pressure and intracranial pressure, attenuated RAAS overactivation, and reduced CNS inflammatory markers — demonstrating a protective cardiovascular effect of estrogen replacement in a menopause-obesity model.

### Use of Transdermal Testosterone to Treat Menopausal Symptoms in Women With a History of Breast Cancer: A Small, Retrospective, Open-Label Study
- **Authors:** Glynne et al.
- **Journal:** Menopause, 2026
- **PMID:** 41941291
- **DOI:** 10.1097/GME.0000000000002777
- **Domain:** Query 4 (menopause + breast cancer) & Query 5 (testosterone/menopause)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Breast cancer survivors on endocrine therapy face particularly difficult menopausal symptoms with very limited hormone options. This retrospective study provides real-world safety and efficacy signals for transdermal testosterone in this high-need group — important context for ALIN's breast cancer survivor content.

> In a UK retrospective cohort of 47 breast cancer survivors (mean age 48.1 y), transdermal testosterone significantly improved menopausal symptom burden as measured by the Menopause Symptom Questionnaire, with no breast cancer recurrences reported during the follow-up period — suggesting testosterone may be a viable option for this population.

### Survival After the First Myocardial Infarction in Older Women: A Prospective Cohort Analysis From the WHI
- **Authors:** Yarahmadi et al.
- **Journal:** Journal of the American Heart Association, 2026
- **PMID:** 41848053
- **DOI:** 10.1161/JAHA.125.046790
- **Domain:** Query 4 (postmenopausal + cardiovascular)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Postmenopausal women are disproportionately affected by MI and its sequelae. This WHI analysis of 5,600 women identifies prognostic factors after a first MI, grounding ALIN messaging about cardiovascular risk awareness and the importance of cardiometabolic vigilance during and after menopause.

> Among 5,600 WHI participants who survived 30 days after an initial MI, survival outcomes were significantly influenced by baseline comorbidities, health behaviours, socioeconomic factors, and psychosocial characteristics — highlighting that menopause-associated cardiovascular risk trajectories are shaped by modifiable lifestyle and social factors.

### Joint and Mediating Effects of Cardiovascular Health in the Association Between Social Determinants of Health and Mortality Among Postmenopausal Women
- **Authors:** Guo et al.
- **Journal:** Maturitas, 2026
- **PMID:** 41950770
- **DOI:** 10.1016/j.maturitas.2026.108932
- **Domain:** Query 4 (postmenopausal + cardiovascular)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Demonstrates that cardiovascular health (Life's Essential 8) mediates the relationship between social determinants and mortality in postmenopausal women — directly relevant to ALIN's equity-aware framing and conversations about lifestyle, access, and health outcomes.

> In 7,577 postmenopausal women from NHANES 2003–2018 (mortality follow-up through 2019), social determinants of health (education, income, food security, neighbourhood) were associated with all-cause mortality, with cardiovascular health (Life's Essential 8 score) acting as a significant mediator — suggesting that improving CVH may partially offset social disadvantage in postmenopausal women.
