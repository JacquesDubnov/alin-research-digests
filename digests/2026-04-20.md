### Neurokinin Receptor Antagonists for Vasomotor Symptoms: From KNDy Neurons to Clinical Translation
- **Authors:** Torres E et al.
- **Journal:** Nature Reviews Endocrinology, 2026
- **PMID:** 41981275
- **DOI:** [10.1038/s41574-026-01247-8](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41574-026-01247-8)
- **Domain:** Query 1 (HRT/MHT) + Query 3 (Non-hormonal/NK3)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Authoritative review from Harvard synthesising the neuroendocrine mechanism behind hot flushes and the clinical translation of NK3R antagonists (fezolinetant, elinzanetant) — directly relevant to ALIN's non-hormonal treatment guidance.

> KNDy neurons in the hypothalamic arcuate nucleus trigger vasomotor symptoms through NK3R-mediated heat dissipation responses during oestrogen withdrawal; hyperactivated KNDy neurons release excessive neurokinin B, driving the flush. NK3R antagonists represent the first non-hormonal class targeting this specific neurobiology, offering promise where hormone therapy is contraindicated.

### The FDA Boxed Warning on Menopausal Hormone Therapy: History, Impact, and a Regulatory Inflection Point in Women's Health
- **Authors:** Buchanan LA et al.
- **Journal:** Menopause, 2026
- **PMID:** 41979564
- **DOI:** [10.1097/GME.0000000000002793](https://doi.org/10.1097/GME.0000000000002793)
- **Domain:** Query 1 (HRT/MHT)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** The November 2025 removal of the FDA black-box warning from MHT products is a landmark regulatory event; ALIN users will encounter both residual fear from the warning era and new clinical conversations, making this historical and policy context essential.

> The class-wide boxed warning—applied after the Women's Health Initiative—drove decades of MHT under-prescribing not supported by post-WHI evidence showing heterogeneity by age, timing, formulation, and route. The authors propose a framework for regulatory reform and clinician re-education.

### Timing and Route of Menopausal Hormone Therapy Critically Shape Structural Brain Outcomes: A Systematic Review of MRI Evidence
- **Authors:** He Z et al.
- **Journal:** Neuroscience and Biobehavioral Reviews, 2026
- **PMID:** 41990931
- **DOI:** [10.1016/j.neubiorev.2026.106684](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neubiorev.2026.106684)
- **Domain:** Query 1 (HRT/MHT) + Query 4 (Cognitive/dementia)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Directly addresses the "timing hypothesis" for brain protection — negative MRI findings were confined to late initiation or oral conjugated equine estrogens, while transdermal estradiol studies showed neutral-to-positive results.

> Systematic review of 24 structural MRI studies: whole-brain analyses showed larger grey matter volumes in frontal/temporal regions in MHT users, but negative findings (reduced hippocampal volume, increased white matter hyperintensities) were consistently limited to late-initiation or oral CEE studies. The authors call for longitudinal RCTs with formulation-specific and timing-specific analyses.

### Prospective Study of Reproductive Span and Menopausal Hormone Therapy and Cognitive Decline over 8 Years in the Nurses' Health Study
- **Authors:** Juramt N et al.
- **Journal:** Menopause, 2026
- **PMID:** 41979543
- **DOI:** [10.1097/GME.0000000000002782](https://doi.org/10.1097/GME.0000000000002782)
- **Domain:** Query 1 (HRT/MHT) + Query 4 (Cognitive)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Large prospective data from the Nurses' Health Study on MHT timing and cognition adds nuance to the "window of opportunity" concept ALIN communicates to users.

> In 14,217 women followed over 8 years: longer reproductive span was associated with better cognitive trajectories. MHT initiated 0–10 years postmenopause was associated with slightly faster decline; use 11+ years postmenopause was not associated. Longer endogenous oestrogen exposure (reproductive span) — not MHT — predicted better trajectories.

### Paradigm Shift in Nonhormonal Treatment Options for Vasomotor Symptoms of Menopause
- **Authors:** Flanagan MR
- **Journal:** Nursing for Women's Health, 2026
- **PMID:** 41997569
- **DOI:** [10.1016/j.nwh.2026.01.004](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.nwh.2026.01.004)
- **Domain:** Query 3 (Non-hormonal/NK3)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Practitioner-facing review covering all three FDA-approved nonhormonal VMS options and the newly approved NK3 antagonists, including benefits beyond VMS (sleep, quality of life) — useful for ALIN's clinical guidance framing.

> Reviews the shift from paroxetine (sole prior FDA-approved nonhormonal option) to neurokinin receptor antagonists (fezolinetant, elinzanetant). NK3 antagonists offer improved sleep and quality of life alongside VMS reduction. Nurses are highlighted as key educators for patients seeking non-hormone approaches.

### Perimenopausal Hot Flashes Do Not Depend on Crude Circulating Oestradiol Levels: Further Evidences from Women Treated with Oral GnRH Antagonists Combination Therapy for Symptomatic Fibroids
- **Authors:** Grandi G et al.
- **Journal:** European Journal of Contraception & Reproductive Health Care, 2026
- **PMID:** 41993011
- **DOI:** [10.1080/13625187.2026.2659158](https://doi.org/10.1080/13625187.2026.2659158)
- **Domain:** Query 2 (Perimenopause)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Challenges the assumption that oestradiol levels predict hot flash severity in perimenopause — stabilisation rather than level matters, with implications for how ALIN explains hot flash mechanisms to users.

> In 7 perimenopausal women on relugolix combination therapy (GnRH antagonist + low-dose oestradiol/NETA for fibroids): hot flash scores improved markedly (VAS 8.6/10) despite significant suppression of FSH, LH, and oestradiol. Improvement likely reflects stabilisation of oestrogen fluctuations, not absolute oestradiol levels.

### Overweight and Obesity Increase Breast Cancer Risk in Postmenopausal Women: A Meta-Analysis of 38 Observational Studies
- **Authors:** Del Console P et al.
- **Journal:** GeroScience, 2026
- **PMID:** 41981355
- **DOI:** [10.1007/s11357-026-02257-0](https://doi.org/10.1007/s11357-026-02257-0)
- **Domain:** Query 4 (Breast cancer)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Confirms and quantifies the menopausal-status-specific breast cancer risk from excess weight, supporting ALIN's lifestyle guidance framing around weight management at menopause.

> Meta-analysis (n = 115,870): in postmenopausal women, overweight conferred OR 1.31 (95% CI 1.13–1.53) and obesity OR 1.43 (95% CI 1.25–1.63) for breast cancer. Associations were non-significant in premenopausal women — menopausal status modulates the adiposity–carcinogenesis link, likely through peripheral oestrogen synthesis in adipose tissue.

### Weight Loss and Omega-3 Supplementation Modulate the Microbiome in Women with Increased Breast Cancer Risk
- **Authors:** Cook KL et al.
- **Journal:** Cancer Prevention Research, 2026
- **PMID:** 41999613
- **DOI:** [10.1158/1940-6207.CAPR-26-0021](https://doi.org/10.1158/1940-6207.CAPR-26-0021)
- **Domain:** Query 4 (Breast cancer)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Pilot RCT in peri/postmenopausal women links high-dose omega-3 plus weight loss to gut microbiome shifts and improved breast cancer risk biomarkers including bioavailable oestradiol — relevant for ALIN's lifestyle and supplement guidance.

> In peri/postmenopausal women with BMI ≥28 kg/m² randomised to omega-3 (3.25 g/day) or placebo during weight loss: omega-3 group showed greatest decrease in Firmicutes/Bacteroidetes ratio, increased SCFA-producing microbes, and improved biomarkers (bioavailable oestradiol, adiponectin-to-leptin ratio, CRP). Effect was greatest in women losing ≥10% body weight.

### Plasma Metabolites, Blood-borne Microvesicles and History of Preeclampsia as Predictors of Coronary Artery Calcification in Postmenopausal Women
- **Authors:** Dokic V et al.
- **Journal:** American Journal of Physiology — Heart and Circulatory Physiology, 2026
- **PMID:** 41973512
- **DOI:** [10.1152/ajpheart.00057.2026](https://doi.org/10.1152/ajpheart.00057.2026)
- **Domain:** Query 4 (Cardiovascular)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Identifies novel biomarker signatures (extracellular vesicles, metabolites) and preeclampsia history as predictors of CAC in postmenopausal women, supporting ALIN's framing that reproductive history shapes midlife CV risk.

> In postmenopausal women (median age 60), CAC was associated with elevated tissue-factor-positive EVs, senescent cell markers, and specific amino acid and carbohydrate metabolites. Women with CAC and a preeclampsia history had 4× greater risk of future CV events or CKD — highlighting sex-specific CV risk factors as ALIN-relevant context.

### Menopause-Related Health Outcomes in Women with Differentiated Thyroid Carcinoma Receiving Long-Term TSH Suppression after Total Thyroidectomy
- **Authors:** Candemir B et al.
- **Journal:** Thyroid, 2026
- **PMID:** 41969134
- **DOI:** [10.1177/10507256261442839](https://doi.org/10.1177/10507256261442839)
- **Domain:** Query 4 (Cardiovascular/quality of life)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Demonstrates that prolonged TSH suppression significantly worsens menopause-specific health (CV health, quality of life, bone, muscle) — important context for ALIN users who are thyroid cancer survivors on suppressive therapy.

> Cross-sectional study (n = 284): postmenopausal women on TSH suppression for ≥3 years showed significantly lower Life's Essential 8 scores, lower Utian QoL scores, higher carotid IMT, higher osteoporosis prevalence, and reduced muscle mass vs. LT4-replacement and euthyroid controls. Cumulative LT4 dose was independently associated with worse outcomes.

### Asparagus racemosus Root Extract (SheVari4®) Alleviates Menopausal Symptoms in Pre-, Peri-, and Post-menopausal Healthy Women
- **Authors:** Swaroop A et al.
- **Journal:** Cureus, 2026
- **PMID:** 41982372
- **DOI:** [10.7759/cureus.106987](https://doi.org/10.7759/cureus.106987)
- **Domain:** Query 1 (HRT/MHT) + Query 2 (Perimenopause)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Small RCT of a standardised Shatavari extract for menopausal symptoms — relevant for ALIN users asking about botanical/non-HRT alternatives, though journal quality (Cureus) warrants noting limitations.

> Double-blind RCT (n = 60, ages 40–55): SheVari4® 100 mg/day vs placebo reduced Menopause Rating Scale scores by 43% at 4 weeks and 71% at 8 weeks (p < 0.001), with >90% improvement in hot flashes and sleep quality. Adverse events were mild and comparable between groups. Note: Cureus is an open-access journal with variable peer-review standards; industry funding should be verified.
