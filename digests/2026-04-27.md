### Adverse Cardiometabolic Impacts of Sleep Fragmentation and Estradiol Suppression: An Experimental Model of Menopause
- **Authors:** Rahman SA et al.
- **Journal:** Journal of Clinical Endocrinology & Metabolism, 2026
- **PMID:** 41206010
- **DOI:** [10.1210/clinem/dgaf592](https://doi.org/10.1210/clinem/dgaf592)
- **Domain:** Query 4 — cardiovascular/cognitive
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Directly quantifies how sleep fragmentation (driven by nocturnal hot flashes) compounds the cardiometabolic harm of estradiol decline, providing mechanistic support for treating both vasomotor symptoms and sleep disruption as cardiovascular risk factors.

> In 38 healthy premenopausal women using a GnRH-agonist menopause model, estradiol suppression worsened lipid profiles and decreased leptin, while sleep fragmentation (without total sleep loss) independently raised heart rate and trended toward raising fasting glucose. Sleep fragmentation worsened a composite cardiometabolic index by an additional 103% over estradiol suppression alone — a modifiable target.

### Subjective Cognition and Objective Cognitive Performance in Early Versus Late Perimenopause
- **Authors:** Furey RT et al.
- **Journal:** Journal of Clinical and Experimental Neuropsychology, 2026
- **PMID:** 42023646
- **DOI:** [10.1080/13803395.2026.2661616](https://doi.org/10.1080/13803395.2026.2661616)
- **Domain:** Query 2 — perimenopause symptoms; Query 4 — cognitive
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Directly investigates menopausal brain fog across perimenopause stages with objective testing, providing data on when cognitive changes emerge and what domains are affected.

> In 687 participants in the Meno-COG online study, women in perimenopause (early + late combined) showed significantly worse learning efficiency than reproductive or postmenopausal women. No difference was found between early and late perimenopause for subjective memory complaints or objective performance, but vasomotor symptoms were significantly higher in late perimenopause. Challenges with STRAW+10 classification highlighted.

### An Exploratory Clinical Study of Structured Psychological Intervention for Menopausal Symptom Management
- **Authors:** Barwick E et al.
- **Journal:** Cureus, 2026
- **PMID:** 42016792
- **DOI:** [10.7759/cureus.107380](https://doi.org/10.7759/cureus.107380)
- **Domain:** Query 2 — perimenopause; Query 4 — cognitive
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Pilot evidence that a single structured non-pharmacological session targeting emotion regulation and cognitive appraisal produces large short-term reductions across psychological, cognitive, and physical menopausal symptoms — relevant to ALIN's therapeutic framework.

> 64 women aged 39–60 with moderate-to-severe menopausal symptoms underwent a single standardised psychological session. At 1- and 4-week follow-up, 89% of symptom domains showed large or very large effect sizes (Cohen's d ≥ 0.5). Psychological and cognitive symptoms showed the greatest improvements; vasomotor symptoms showed moderate effects.

### Ultra-Processed Food Intake and Postmenopausal Breast Cancer Risk in the NIH-AARP Diet and Health Study
- **Authors:** O'Connell CP et al.
- **Journal:** Journal of the National Cancer Institute, 2026
- **PMID:** 42015373
- **DOI:** [10.1093/jnci/djag124](https://doi.org/10.1093/jnci/djag124)
- **Domain:** Query 4 — breast cancer
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Directly addresses a common user concern (diet and breast cancer risk) with a large prospective dataset, showing no independent association between ultra-processed food and postmenopausal breast cancer after careful adjustment.

> In 181,460 postmenopausal women followed for a median 21 years (NIH-AARP cohort), 14,484 breast cancers were diagnosed. No association was observed between UPF intake and breast cancer overall, by ER status, or by tumour type. Inverse trends after BMI adjustment were attributed to over-adjustment. Obesity, not UPF per se, likely mediates any diet–cancer link.

### Estrogen Replacement Therapy and Physical Function in Rheumatoid Arthritis
- **Authors:** Mollard E et al.
- **Journal:** Rheumatology (Oxford), 2026
- **PMID:** 42032824
- **DOI:** [10.1093/rheumatology/keag197](https://doi.org/10.1093/rheumatology/keag197)
- **Domain:** Query 1 — HRT/MHT; Query 4 — cardiovascular
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Adds real-world evidence that ERT modestly improves physical function (HAQ score) in postmenopausal women with RA, with greatest benefit when initiated within 5 years of menopause — reinforces the timing-window principle for HRT and extends it to an autoimmune population.

> Longitudinal analysis of 8,246 matched postmenopausal women with RA (Forward database, 2000–2022). ERT use associated with modestly improved HAQ scores (β = −0.02, p<0.001). Timing effect: greater benefit with ERT initiated within 5 years of menopause. Propensity-score analyses confirmed findings. No excess crude rates of cardiovascular or cancer events observed.

### A Randomized Controlled Trial on the Effect of Gushukang on Bone Mineral Density in Perimenopausal and Postmenopausal Women
- **Authors:** Liu L et al.
- **Journal:** Gynecological Endocrinology, 2026
- **PMID:** 42033017
- **DOI:** [10.1080/09513590.2026.2659980](https://doi.org/10.1080/09513590.2026.2659980)
- **Domain:** Query 1 — HRT/MHT; Query 2 — perimenopause
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Provides RCT data on combining traditional Chinese medicine (Gushukang) with HT for bone health in perimenopause — relevant for users in East Asian healthcare contexts or exploring integrative approaches.

> 156 women (52 per group) randomised to HT alone, Gushukang (TCM) alone, or combination for 6 months. BMD increased significantly in the combination group vs. baseline (p<0.01). Both HT and combination reduced FSH/LH and raised E2; Gushukang alone showed no hormonal change. No liver or kidney toxicity in any group.

### Soy Isoflavone Supplementation and Sexual Function in Postmenopausal Women: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis
- **Authors:** Yildiz Karaahmet A, Sadeghi E
- **Journal:** International Journal of Impotence Research, 2026
- **PMID:** 42032055
- **DOI:** [10.1038/s41443-026-01278-9](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41443-026-01278-9)
- **Domain:** Query 1 — HRT/MHT
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Addresses a very common question from users considering phytoestrogen supplements for vaginal/sexual symptoms, with quantified effect sizes.

> Meta-analysis of 13 RCTs (n=1,325). Soy isoflavones (40–160 mg/day) significantly improved vaginal dryness (SMD = −1.88) and urogenital symptoms (SMD = −0.33). Dose-dependent effects, optimal at 50–100 mg/day; effects more pronounced in Asian populations. Non-significant trends for vasomotor and psychological symptoms. High heterogeneity (I²>90%) noted.

### Symptoms Experienced During the Menopause Transition and Cognition in Older Women With Cardiovascular Disease
- **Authors:** Wilbur J et al.
- **Journal:** Journal of Cardiovascular Nursing, 2026
- **PMID:** 42007744
- **DOI:** [10.1097/JCN.0000000000001304](https://doi.org/10.1097/JCN.0000000000001304)
- **Domain:** Query 1 — HRT/MHT; Query 4 — cardiovascular/cognitive
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Cross-sectional data from 197 older women with CVD shows that somatic symptoms during the menopause transition (and HRT use) are associated with later-life episodic memory — supporting ALIN's framing of menopause as a whole-body event with long-term cognitive implications.

> Secondary analysis of MindMoves trial baseline data (n=197 women with CVD, no bilateral oophorectomy before FMP). Fewer somatic menopause transition symptoms and ever having used HRT were associated with higher episodic memory. More sexual dysfunction was associated with higher working memory. No biomarker associations found.

### Reduction of Glycation Stress as a Geroscience Intervention: Protocol for a Pilot RCT in Postmenopausal Women (GRACE Trial)
- **Authors:** Tanwar V et al.
- **Journal:** npj Aging, 2026
- **PMID:** 42026060
- **DOI:** [10.1038/s41514-026-00373-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41514-026-00373-x)
- **Domain:** Query 4 — cardiovascular/metabolic
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Describes a novel geroscience approach for postmenopausal metabolic decline — targeting advanced glycation end-products (AGEs) which accumulate faster after menopause. An emerging area relevant to ALIN's nutrition and aging content.

> GRACE trial protocol: double-blind, placebo-controlled 6-month pilot RCT testing GLYLO (alpha-lipoic acid + nicotinamide + pyridoxine + benfotiamine + piperine) vs. placebo in 45–65-year-old postmenopausal women with elevated adiposity and pre-diabetic HbA1c. Primary endpoint: reduction in circulating AGEs and methylglyoxal. Secondary endpoints include cognition, body composition, and systemic inflammation.

### Estrogen Deprivation Induces Hepatic Inflammation, IDO1, Tryptophan Catabolism, and Plasma Cholesterol
- **Authors:** Guha P et al.
- **Journal:** Scientific Reports, 2026
- **PMID:** 42031929
- **DOI:** [10.1038/s41598-026-48938-w](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-026-48938-w)
- **Domain:** Query 4 — cardiovascular
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Provides mechanistic insight into how estrogen loss drives cardiometabolic risk via a hepatic inflammation → IDO1 → kynurenine → cholesterol pathway, potentially explaining why postmenopausal women show worsening lipid profiles.

> In ovariectomized Long-Evans rats, estrogen deprivation raised LDL and total cholesterol, reduced HDL, elevated hepatic TNFα/IL-6/IDO1/TDO2, and increased plasma kynurenine, nitric oxide, and lactate. E2 replacement reversed all these changes. Targeted metabolomics showed enhanced Trp→kynurenine flux restored by estradiol.

### Prevalence of ASCVD Risk-Enhancing Factors and Their Association with Primary Prevention Statin Use
- **Authors:** Wilson LM et al.
- **Journal:** Journal of General Internal Medicine, 2026
- **PMID:** 42010102
- **DOI:** [10.1007/s11606-026-10423-5](https://doi.org/10.1007/s11606-026-10423-5)
- **Domain:** Query 4 — cardiovascular
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Premature menopause is formally recognised as an ASCVD risk-enhancing factor in ACC/AHA guidelines; this real-world NHANES analysis shows it remains under-utilised in clinical statin prescribing decisions — relevant for ALIN users discussing cardiovascular risk with their clinicians.

> In a nationally representative sample of 3,111 adults (52.6% female), 77% had ≥1 ASCVD risk-enhancing factor (REF); 28% had ≥3. Premature menopause is among the nine REFs measured. - **Relevance to ALIN:** This large GWAS (n=544,513) finds that PCOS risk loci are associated with *later* age at menopause, linking the lifespan reproductive biology of testosterone and ovarian reserve to menopause timing — background science for ALIN content on hormonal history and menopause age.

### Genetic Association Between Sex Hormones, Serum Urate, and Gout: A Comprehensive Mendelian Randomization Study
- **Authors:** Li S et al.
- **Journal:** International Journal of Endocrinology, 2026
- **PMID:** 42016019
- **DOI:** [10.1155/ije/8839727](https://doi.org/10.1155/ije/8839727)
- **Domain:** Query 5 — testosterone/sex hormones
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Provides MR evidence that testosterone genetically increases serum urate in women, with androstenediol mediating 28.4% of the effect — relevant context for ALIN content on testosterone therapy and metabolic health in postmenopausal women.

> Two-sample MR in European GWAS data (n up to ~544K). Genetically predicted total testosterone was causally associated with higher serum urate in women (β=0.035, p=0.018). Age at menopause was also associated with urate. Mediation analysis identified androstenediol (3β,17β) disulfate as a partial mediator. Findings suggest sex-specific tailoring of hyperuricemia interventions.
