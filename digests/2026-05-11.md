## New Findings

### Report of the FDA's Expert Panel on Hormone Therapy
- **Authors:** Pinkerton JV et al.
- **Journal:** Menopause, 2026
- **PMID:** 42084927
- **DOI:** [10.1097/GME.0000000000002768](https://doi.org/10.1097/GME.0000000000002768)
- **Domain:** Query 1 — HRT/MHT/menopause
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Directly foundational — this summarises the FDA's July 2025 expert panel and the November 2025 decision to remove boxed warnings from estrogen therapies. ALIN's hormone therapy guidance will need to reflect this regulatory shift, especially around vaginal estrogen safety messaging.

> "On November 10, 2025, the FDA recommended removing boxed warnings from estrogen therapies, with specific, nuanced considerations." The panel emphasized differences between systemic and local vaginal estrogen and risks of class labeling.

### Recent Advances in the Relationship Between Mental Symptoms in Postmenopausal Women and Estrogen Fluctuations
- **Authors:** Zhu J et al.
- **Journal:** Journal of Psychosomatic Obstetrics and Gynaecology, 2026
- **PMID:** 42100911
- **DOI:** [10.1080/0167482X.2026.2666107](https://doi.org/10.1080/0167482X.2026.2666107)
- **Domain:** Query 1 — HRT/MHT/menopause
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Depression and anxiety are among the most under-addressed menopause symptoms in clinical practice; this review of mechanisms (HPA axis, neuroinflammation, epigenetics) and MHT efficacy supports ALIN's mental health content.

> "Depression rates in menopausal women are 2–3 times higher than in premenopausal women, primarily due to rapid estrogen changes." MHT can restore neurotransmitter balance but requires personalization.

### Later Menopause Confers No Additional Protection Against Osteoporosis in Older Women
- **Authors:** Zhou Z et al.
- **Journal:** Endocrine Connections, 2026
- **PMID:** 42101469
- **DOI:** [10.1530/EC-26-0078](https://doi.org/10.1530/EC-26-0078)
- **Domain:** Query 1 — HRT/MHT/menopause
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Challenges the assumption that late menopause is protective for bone; confirms that BMI and hormone use history are the two key modifiable factors for osteoporosis risk — useful for ALIN's bone health module.

> "The initial protective advantage in reducing osteoporosis risk conferred by a later age at menopause diminishes in later life. Maintaining a higher BMI and appropriate hormone therapy can effectively compensate."

### Peri- and Post-Menopausal Hormone Replacement Therapy and Voice Disorder Risk: A TriNetX Study
- **Authors:** Kayekjian D et al.
- **Journal:** The Laryngoscope, 2026
- **PMID:** 42092344
- **DOI:** [10.1002/lary.70612](https://doi.org/10.1002/lary.70612)
- **Domain:** Query 1 — HRT/MHT/menopause
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Voice changes are a commonly reported but rarely discussed menopause symptom; this large TriNetX cohort study (n=265,311) provides reassurance that HRT has no significant long-term clinical impact on voice.

> "HRT may not have any significant clinical impact on the peri- and post-menopausal voice. Although higher odds of dysphonia were noted earlier after treatment, HRT users did not have higher odds of voice issues after 1 year."

### The Effect of Auricular Acupressure on Quality of Life in Postmenopausal Women: A Randomized Sham-Controlled Clinical Trial
- **Authors:** Alimoradi Z et al.
- **Journal:** Climacteric, 2026
- **PMID:** 42095404
- **DOI:** [10.1080/13697137.2026.2645641](https://doi.org/10.1080/13697137.2026.2645641)
- **Domain:** Query 1 — HRT/MHT/menopause (non-hormonal)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Adds to ALIN's non-pharmacological options library; this RCT (n=120) showed auricular acupressure significantly improved all MENQOL domains over 2 months, relevant for MHT-ineligible users.

> "Auricular acupressure significantly improved overall QoL and all specific domains (physical, psychosocial, vasomotor, sexual) in postmenopausal women." Effect size accounted for 63.4% of total QoL variance.

### Critical Implications of Female Bone Metabolism in Orthopedics: State of the Art
- **Authors:** Hecht S et al.
- **Journal:** Journal of ISAKOS, 2026
- **PMID:** 42106009
- **DOI:** [10.1016/j.jisako.2026.101133](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jisako.2026.101133)
- **Domain:** Queries 1 & 2 — HRT/MHT; perimenopause
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Comprehensive review linking perimenopause, bone loss, and orthopedic outcomes (fracture fixation, joint arthroplasty); supports ALIN content on musculoskeletal health, especially for athletic and active users.

> "Estrogen deficiency—most notably during the menopausal transition—leads to accelerated bone loss, deterioration of bone microarchitecture, and a marked increase in osteoporotic fracture risk. Menopausal hormone therapy has demonstrated efficacy in preventing bone loss and reducing fracture incidence."

- **Journal:** Rheumatology (Oxford), 2026
- **PMID:** 42032824
- **DOI:** [10.1093/rheumatology/keag197](https://doi.org/10.1093/rheumatology/keag197)
- **Domain:** Queries 1 & 4 — HRT/MHT; cardiovascular/musculoskeletal

### Cardiovascular Risk in Women with Metabolic Dysfunction-Associated Steatotic Liver Disease Across the Menopausal Transition
- **Authors:** Choi YJ et al.
- **Journal:** European Journal of Preventive Cardiology, 2026
- **PMID:** 42084338
- **DOI:** [10.1093/eurjpc/zwag254](https://doi.org/10.1093/eurjpc/zwag254)
- **Domain:** Query 4 — cardiovascular
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Large Korean cohort (n=1.9M) quantifying MASLD-related MACE risk stratified by menopausal status; postmenopausal women face more than double the absolute CV excess risk from liver fat — important for ALIN's cardiometabolic content.

> "The absolute excess risk associated with MASLD was larger in postmenopausal women (0.51%–1.09% absolute risk difference) than in premenopausal women (0.20%–0.42%)." Emphasizes need to incorporate hepatic steatosis and menopausal status in CV risk assessment.

- **Authors:** Woods NF et al.
- **Journal:** Menopause, 2026
- **PMID:** 42084943
- **DOI:** [10.1097/GME.0000000000002783](https://doi.org/10.1097/GME.0000000000002783)
- **Domain:** Query 4 — cognitive/wellbeing
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Longitudinal data from SMWHS (n=261) confirms women's health self-ratings dip specifically at the late menopausal transition and early postmenopause — validating ALIN's focus on this window as a critical intervention point.

### Sexual Health among Patients with Breast Cancer Undergoing Endocrine Therapy: An Integrative Review

- **Journal:** Supportive Care in Cancer, 2026
- **PMID:** 42081187
- **DOI:** [10.1007/s00520-026-10746-3](https://doi.org/10.1007/s00520-026-10746-3)
- **Domain:** Queries 4 & 5 — breast cancer; genitourinary syndrome
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Integrative review of 43 studies documenting 51–93% prevalence of sexual dysfunction in breast cancer patients on endocrine therapy, with GSM as the dominant complaint — directly relevant to ALIN's content for breast cancer survivors.
