### Vaginal Estrogen Use Reduces Serious Adverse Outcomes in Women With Recurrent UTI
- **Authors:** LaClair J et al.
- **Journal:** Urology, 2026
- **PMID:** 42269780
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.urology.2026.06.004
- **Domain:** Query 5 (testosterone/DHEA/vaginal estrogen/GSM)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** This massive real-world study (n=1.89 million) quantifies a dramatic protective effect of vaginal estrogen on serious outcomes in recurrent UTI — directly relevant to counseling perimenopausal and postmenopausal users about GSM treatment benefits beyond symptom relief.

> In 1.89 million women with recurrent UTI, vaginal estrogen prescription was associated with markedly lower odds of sepsis (OR 0.22–0.46 across age groups), hospitalization (OR 0.31–0.62), and all-cause mortality (OR 0.20–0.38) compared to non-recipients. The benefit was consistent across all age groups (20–99 years).

### Is Vaginal Estrogen Treatment Associated with Risk of Thrombosis in Postmenopausal Women? A Scoping Review
- **Authors:** Juul SL et al.
- **Journal:** Seminars in Thrombosis and Hemostasis, 2026
- **PMID:** 42285171
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1055/a-2882-1241
- **Domain:** Queries 4 & 5 (cardiovascular/VTE and vaginal estrogen)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Directly addresses a common concern among ALIN users about vaginal estrogen safety; the evidence points to no increased thrombotic risk, which is important for messaging around GSM treatment.

> This scoping review of 11 observational studies found no increased risk of VTE, myocardial infarction, or stroke associated with vaginal estrogen use in postmenopausal women. Effect estimates ranged from null to reduced risk for all thrombotic outcomes. One study even showed reduced risk of recurrent MI after discontinuing vaginal estrogen.

### Genitourinary Syndrome of Menopause in Surgical Versus Natural Menopause: Standardized Clinical Scoring
- **Authors:** Ozmen S et al.
- **Journal:** Menopause, 2026
- **PMID:** 42262430
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1097/GME.0000000000002833
- **Domain:** Query 5 (vaginal estrogen/GSM)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Quantifies GSM severity differences between surgical and natural menopause — important context for ALIN users who have had oophorectomy and may experience more severe genitourinary symptoms.

> In 422 postmenopausal women (218 surgical, 204 natural), surgical menopause was independently associated with higher total GSM scores (adjusted OR 1.08 per point, 95% CI 1.04–1.12). Genital dryness, dyspareunia, reduced sexual desire, postcoital bleeding, dysuria, and urinary frequency were all significantly more common after surgical menopause.

### Psychosocial Interventions for Physiological Symptoms of Menopause: Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis
- **Authors:** Robinson K et al.
- **Journal:** BMC Women's Health, 2026
- **PMID:** 42252432
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1186/s12905-026-04563-3
- **Domain:** Queries 1 & 4 (HRT/menopause and cognitive/dementia)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** This meta-analysis of 28 RCTs (n=2,887) is one of the most comprehensive assessments of CBT and other psychosocial approaches for menopausal symptoms — highly actionable for ALIN's non-pharmacological content and counseling features.

> Psychosocial interventions produced medium-to-large reductions in hot flush bothersomeness (Hedges' g = −0.60 to −0.87 short-term), strong improvements in sleep quality (g = −0.77 to −1.04) and insomnia (g = −1.77 to −2.48 short-term). Effects on sexual functioning and urogenital symptoms were not significant. Retention rates were high (mean 86.7%).

- **PMID:** 42261852
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1080/13697137.2026.2661353

- **Relevance to ALIN:** RCT evidence for culturally adapted group CBT reducing VMS and improving quality of life, anxiety, and sleep; supports ALIN's potential to provide evidence-based behavioural interventions across diverse populations.

### MHT in Borderline Ovarian Tumor Survivors: Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis
- **Authors:** Mejia-Gomez J et al.
- **Journal:** Climacteric, 2026
- **PMID:** 42258345
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1080/13697137.2026.2675561
- **Domain:** Query 1 (HRT/menopause)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Addresses a gap in the evidence for a specific high-need population; combined MHT showed a modest association with higher odds of BOTs (OR 1.43), but postsurgical safety data are sparse — important nuance for ALIN's risk-communication features.

> Meta-analysis of 11 studies (4 cohort, 7 case-control): combined estrogen-progestin MHT showed OR 1.43 (95% CI 1.08–1.88) for BOT occurrence; estrogen-only MHT showed no significant association. No study addressed BOT recurrence; certainty of evidence rated very low. Multidisciplinary individualised decision-making recommended.

### Multiple Recurrences of Postmenopausal Endometriosis Associated with Estrogen Pellet Therapy
- **Authors:** Wu E et al.
- **Journal:** Menopause, 2026
- **PMID:** 42262419
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1097/GME.0000000000002832
- **Domain:** Query 1 (HRT/menopause)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Case report + narrative review illustrating risks of supraphysiologic/unopposed estrogen via pellets in postmenopausal women with history of endometriosis — relevant to ALIN's messaging on hormone therapy formulation choices.

> 70-year-old postmenopausal woman with prior BSO and confirmed endometriosis experienced repeated recurrences over two decades while on subcutaneous estrogen pellet therapy. Review supports progestogen add-back regardless of uterine status in women with endometriosis history, and highlights concern about pellet-delivered supraphysiologic estrogen levels.

### Early Menopause and Cardiovascular Disease Risk: Triangulating the Evidence (Combined Analysis in East Asian Women)
- **Authors:** Wang Y et al.
- **Journal:** Climacteric, 2026
- **PMID:** 42253047
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1080/13697137.2026.2675562
- **Domain:** Queries 1 & 4 (HRT/menopause and cardiovascular)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** The first study combining observational and Mendelian randomization evidence in Asian women; the dose-response relationship between menopausal age and CVD risk has implications for personalizing cardiovascular risk counseling in ALIN.

> In 1,929 Chinese postmenopausal women (7-year follow-up), early menopause was associated with HR 1.43 (95% CI 1.05–1.97) for CVD. However, two-sample Mendelian randomization in China Kadoorie Biobank/Biobank Japan did not confirm causality, suggesting confounding. A dose-response relationship between age at menopause and CVD risk was observed (p-nonlinearity = 0.022).

### Postmenopausal Osteoporosis, Endothelial Dysfunction, and Cardiovascular Risk
- **Authors:** Pickering ME et al.
- **Journal:** Journal of Clinical Rheumatology, 2026
- **PMID:** 42257649
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1097/RHU.0000000000002352
- **Domain:** Query 4 (cardiovascular)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Demonstrates that cardiovascular risk may be underestimated in women classified as "low/intermediate risk" by standard SCORE2 — findings support ALIN's holistic approach to menopause health monitoring.

> In 111 postmenopausal women with osteoporosis (mean age 69.4y), 55% classified as low/intermediate cardiovascular risk had low flow-mediated dilation (FMD), suggesting vascular impairment beyond traditional risk scores. Prior bisphosphonate use was positively associated with FMD. Findings support integrating endothelial function assessment in osteoporotic women.

### Brain Creatine, Estradiol, and Neurocognitive Complaints in Perimenopausal Women
- **Authors:** Ostojic SM et al.
- **Journal:** Neuroscience Letters, 2026
- **PMID:** 42061673
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neulet.2026.138616
- **Domain:** Query 2 (perimenopause/symptoms/treatment)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Provides neurobiological evidence for brain energy deficits underlying cognitive complaints in perimenopause — supports ALIN's content on brain health and the rationale for early hormonal and lifestyle interventions.

> In 12 perimenopausal women, whole-brain creatine (6.31 mM) was significantly lower than reference values in younger adults (p=0.049). Lower thalamic and parietal creatine correlated with concentration difficulties (τ = −0.38 to −0.51). Serum estradiol was inversely associated with right parietal gray-matter creatine, linking estrogen decline to brain bioenergetic changes.

### Perimenopausal Migraine: A Narrative Review
- **Authors:** Kamourieh S, MacGregor EA
- **Journal:** Climacteric, 2026
- **PMID:** 42273725
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1080/13697137.2026.2675559
- **Domain:** Query 2 (perimenopause/symptoms)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Migraine is a common and under-recognized perimenopausal symptom; this review provides actionable guidance on hormonal contraception and MHT choices that ALIN could translate into personalized symptom management content.

> Hyperestrogenism in early perimenopause worsens migraine frequency and severity. There are no RCTs of MHT specifically in perimenopausal migraineurs, but observational data suggest MHT may worsen migraine by adding to already-high fluctuating estrogen. Continuous combined hormonal contraception (migraine without aura) or progestogen-only regimens with optional transdermal estrogen (migraine with/without aura) are preferred during perimenopause.

### Low-Fat Dietary Pattern and Dementia Mortality: Secondary Analysis of WHI DM Trial (20-Year Follow-Up)
- **Authors:** Chlebowski RT et al.
- **Journal:** Menopause, 2026
- **PMID:** 42262514
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1097/GME.0000000000002808
- **Domain:** Query 4 (cognitive/dementia)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Null finding from the largest dietary RCT in postmenopausal women — important for calibrating ALIN's dietary guidance on dementia prevention; diet quality matters but low-fat alone is not sufficient.

> In 48,835 postmenopausal women randomised to low-fat vs. usual diet with 20-year follow-up (WHI Dietary Modification trial), dietary intervention did not reduce dementia mortality overall (HR 0.94, 95% CI 0.85–1.05). A trend for lower dementia mortality in the youngest subgroup (50–59y, HR 0.73) was not statistically significant.

### Probiotic Supplementation and Bone Mineral Density in Early Postmenopausal Women: 12-Month RCT (Negative Finding)
- **Authors:** Resciniti S et al.
- **Journal:** American Journal of Clinical Nutrition, 2026
- **PMID:** 42264450
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ajcnut.2026.101384
- **Domain:** Query 4 (postmenopausal/cardiovascular/bone)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Well-designed RCT showing a lactobacilli probiotic did NOT prevent bone loss — and may have slightly increased loss at the primary site; helps ALIN counsel against unsubstantiated probiotic use for bone health.

> In 114 women 1–8 years postmenopause randomised to lactobacilli-based probiotic vs. placebo for 12 months, the probiotic group showed a small but significantly greater decline in distal tibia volumetric BMD (−3.7 mg HA/cm³, 95% CI −6.9 to −0.4, p=0.027). No benefit was seen for bone turnover markers or other skeletal sites.

### High-Intensity Interval Nordic Walking Improves Cardiorespiratory Fitness and Reduces Inflammation in Postmenopausal Women
- **Authors:** Tan L et al.
- **Journal:** BMC Sports Science, Medicine & Rehabilitation, 2026
- **PMID:** 42265787
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1186/s13102-026-01798-3
- **Domain:** Query 4 (postmenopausal/cardiovascular)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Demonstrates that a 12-week accessible exercise program (Nordic walking at high intensity) significantly improves cardiovascular fitness and inflammatory markers in postmenopausal women with overweight/obesity — translatable to ALIN lifestyle recommendations.

> 45 postmenopausal women (age ~70y, BMI ~28) assigned to 3x/week HIIT Nordic walking vs. control. After 12 weeks: greater VO2max (36.3 vs. 32.2 mL/kg/min, p<0.01), lower CRP, TNF-α, IL-6, and higher adiponectin (all p<0.05) in the exercise group. Improvements in VO2max correlated negatively with IL-6 changes.
