### Progestogen Monotherapy as Menopausal Hormone Therapy: A Narrative Review
- **Authors:** Thomas C et al.
- **Journal:** BJOG: An International Journal of Obstetrics and Gynaecology, 2026
- **PMID:** 42347558
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1111/1471-0528.70292
- **Domain:** Query 1 — HRT/MHT + menopause
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Directly relevant for users who cannot take estrogen-containing MHT (prior VTE, coronary artery disease, compensated liver disease, select gynecologic malignancies). Supports personalized treatment guidance.

> Progestogen monotherapy—particularly micronized progesterone—is an effective, well-tolerated alternative MHT for women with estrogen contraindications. Micronized progesterone offers additional sleep benefits; NETA and MPA variably confer bone protection. Breast cancer risk data remain underpowered. Large comparative trials vs. standard MHT are still lacking.

### Menopause and Its Implications for Stroke in Women
- **Authors:** Thurston RC et al.
- **Journal:** Stroke, 2026
- **PMID:** 42339545
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1161/STROKEAHA.126.052889
- **Domain:** Query 1 / Query 4 — HRT + cardiovascular
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Synthesizes how menopause type, timing, VMS, sleep disruption, and MHT formulation/route each modulate stroke risk — actionable for ALIN's HRT guidance and risk-communication features.

> Menopause is a major driver of stroke risk in women. Transdermal MHT formulations in younger midlife women show a more favorable stroke-risk profile than oral formulations. VMS and sleep disturbance are independently associated with elevated risk. The review highlights formulation- and timing-specific clinical guidance gaps.

### Complex Benign Gynecology in Perimenopause: Current Evidence and Future Directions
- **Authors:** Goren R et al.
- **Journal:** Current Opinion in Obstetrics & Gynecology, 2026
- **PMID:** 42306911
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1097/GCO.0000000000001124
- **Domain:** Query 2 / Query 4 — perimenopause + cardiovascular
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Perimenopause is when fibroids, AUB, and endometriosis peak and intersect with proximity to natural menopause. ALIN users frequently ask about surgical timing and ovarian conservation.

> Oophorectomy before age 45–50 is associated with worse long-term cardiovascular outcomes. Opportunistic salpingectomy during indicated benign surgery is supported for ovarian cancer prevention while preserving hormonal function. Surgical management must be individualized against proximity to menopause and malignancy risk.

### Hormonal Shifts and Structural Strain: Menopause and Perimenopause in Relation to Overuse Injuries and Stress Fractures
- **Authors:** Ansari M et al.
- **Journal:** Annals of Rehabilitation Medicine, 2026
- **PMID:** 42332940
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.5535/arm.250134
- **Domain:** Query 1 / Query 3 — HRT + non-hormonal options
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Musculoskeletal symptoms and injury risk are common ALIN user concerns; this review proposes a hormone-tissue-load mismatch model with practical prevention strategies.

> Early MHT initiation reduces fracture risk by 20–40%. Estrogen decline causes 2–5% annual bone loss, impairs tendon repair, and increases ligament laxity during perimenopause. Non-hormonal strategies (resistance training, DXA screening, nutritional optimization) remain underutilized. Authors propose a hormone-informed exercise prescription framework.

### Evaluation of Photobiomodulation Therapy for Genitourinary Syndrome of Menopause
- **Authors:** Wu P-C et al.
- **Journal:** PLoS ONE, 2026
- **PMID:** 42329990
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0351765
- **Domain:** Query 3 / Query 5 — non-hormonal + GSM
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Adds to ALIN's non-hormonal GSM treatment library; relevant for users who decline or cannot use estrogen. Well-tolerated, with 3-month durability data.

> Eight weekly 30-minute sessions of intravaginal 660-nm photobiomodulation (PBM) in 27 postmenopausal women with GSM produced significant improvements in Vaginal Health Index, lower urinary tract symptoms, and sexual function scores sustained at 3 months. All participants reached non-atrophic VHI threshold. No adverse events. Only 13% achieved functional FSFI recovery threshold.

### Gut, Vaginal, and Urinary Microbiome Alterations in Women with Genitourinary Syndrome of Menopause: A Systematic Review
- **Authors:** Tsuboi I et al.
- **Journal:** Maturitas, 2026
- **PMID:** 42341424
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.maturitas.2026.109031
- **Domain:** Query 5 — GSM + vaginal estrogen
- **Relevance to ALIN:** First systematic review to synthesize evidence across all three microbiome compartments in GSM — supports ALIN's emerging content on the vaginal-bladder-gut axis.

> Across 23 studies (5,027 participants), postmenopausal GSM is consistently characterised by Lactobacillus depletion and increased microbial diversity. Specific pathogens linked to symptom domains: Prevotella → urinary symptoms; Finegoldia magna → recurrent UTI; Streptococcus → sexual pain. Estrogen therapy partially restores Lactobacillus but does not uniformly resolve symptoms. Evidence points to a shared vaginal-bladder-gut axis likely mediated by the estrobolome.

### Survival Outcomes in Breast Cancer Patients With Use of Vaginal Estrogen Therapy: A SEER Analysis
- **Authors:** Mitchel OR et al.
- **Journal:** JCO Oncology Practice, 2026
- **PMID:** 42361283
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1200/OP-25-01142
- **Domain:** Query 4 / Query 5 — breast cancer + vaginal estrogen
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Directly applicable to the large proportion of ALIN users who are breast cancer survivors hesitant about local vaginal estrogen; largest real-world SEER-Medicare analysis to date.

> In 25,874 female breast cancer patients ≥65 years, vaginal estrogen use (n=1,053) was not associated with worse overall or cause-specific mortality in time-dependent analyses. Initial unadjusted associations likely reflect immortal time bias. Findings support the growing consensus that local vaginal estrogen is safe in appropriately selected breast cancer survivors.

### Redefining Testosterone Reference Ranges for Adult Females
- **Authors:** Wainwright E et al.
- **Journal:** Journal of Endocrinological Investigation, 2026
- **PMID:** 42340622
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1007/s40618-026-02929-w
- **Domain:** Query 5 — testosterone + women
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Addresses the clinical gap in female testosterone diagnostics; provides the largest age-continuous reference dataset, directly supporting ALIN's testosterone therapy guidance.

> In 5,323 women aged 19–59 with normal BMI, regular cycles, and no reproductive conditions, testosterone follows a weakly skew-Normal distribution with mean decreasing and variance increasing linearly with age. Highlights a substantial decline in testosterone across adult life. Provides a validated age-continuous reference range tool for differentiating physiological from pathological states.

### Perioperative Vaginal Estrogen and Prolapse Surgery Results: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis
- **Authors:** Vacca L et al.
- **Journal:** Climacteric, 2026
- **PMID:** 42338418
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1080/13697137.2026.2671751
- **Domain:** Query 5 — vaginal estrogen + postmenopausal
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Relevant for postmenopausal ALIN users planning pelvic floor surgery — provides evidence-based guidance on perioperative vaginal estrogen.

> Meta-analysis of 9 RCTs (764 women) demonstrates significant tissue quality improvement (SMD 0.73) with perioperative vaginal estrogen. However, no significant difference in composite surgical success or UTI prevention. Vaginal atrophy symptoms improved significantly. Safety profiles were excellent. Supports use for tissue optimization and symptom management, not for surgical success enhancement.

### Comparative Study of DHEA vs. Femarelle® on Menopausal Systemic and Genitourinary Syndrome
- **Authors:** Long C-Y et al.
- **Journal:** Gynecologic and Obstetric Investigation, 2026
- **PMID:** 42328990
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1159/000552775
- **Domain:** Query 5 — DHEA + menopause/GSM
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Head-to-head comparison of two non-estrogen GSM options that ALIN users frequently ask about; clinically useful for individualized treatment selection.

> In 70 postmenopausal women (mean age 53), both DHEA (60 mg/day) and Femarelle® (644 mg/day) reduced total climacteric scores comparably. DHEA was superior for urinary symptoms (OABSS, UDI-6, IIQ-7, ICIQ) and sexual function (desire, arousal, lubrication, orgasm, satisfaction). No significant difference in serum estradiol or FSH between groups. Testosterone was not measured—a key limitation.

### Sex-Specific Hormone Effects on Cerebrovascular Health Across the Menopausal Transition
- **Authors:** Intzandt B et al.
- **Journal:** Mechanisms of Ageing and Development, 2026
- **PMID:** 42361914
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.mad.2026.112216
- **Domain:** Query 5 — testosterone/estrogen + menopause/postmenopausal
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Provides neuroimaging evidence linking the menopausal hormone transition to cerebrovascular changes — supports ALIN's brain health content and the rationale for timing-based hormone interventions.

> In 187 females (pre/peri/postmenopausal): estradiol was associated with lower arterial transit time during perimenopause. Testosterone showed a U-shaped relationship with cerebrovascular reactivity (CVR) in premenopausal women, but lower cerebral blood flow and CVR in postmenopausal females — suggesting stage-specific protective vs. detrimental roles. Emphasizes the need for life-stage-specific hormone interventions.

### Effect of Parity and β-Amyloid on Cognition and Hippocampal Volume in Postmenopausal Women
- **Authors:** Gallay C et al.
- **Journal:** Neurology, 2026
- **PMID:** 42330435
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1212/WNL.0000000000218153
- **Domain:** Query 4 — cognitive + postmenopausal
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Introduces parity as a modifiable reproductive history factor that may affect Alzheimer's resilience in postmenopausal women — relevant to ALIN's dementia risk communication.

> In 254 cognitively unimpaired postmenopausal women (ALFA+ cohort), higher parity interacted with amyloid-positive status to produce steeper cognitive decline (β = −0.035) and lower hippocampal volume over ~3 years. Among amyloid-negative women, parity showed no significant association. Suggests parity may reduce resilience to Alzheimer's pathology in preclinical stages.

### Mapping Menopause Education for Healthcare Professionals: A Scoping Review
- **Authors:** Keye C et al.
- **Journal:** Climacteric, 2026
- **PMID:** 42329785
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1080/13697137.2026.2676683
- **Domain:** Query 1 — HRT + menopause (education context)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Documents the systemic gap in HCP menopause training — validates ALIN's role in bridging care gaps for users whose providers lack adequate menopause knowledge.

> Scoping review of 14 studies (mostly US, mostly medical residents) finds menopause education is inconsistently embedded across health disciplines. Interventions reliably improve knowledge and confidence, but long-term health implications and equity-focused content are rarely included. Standardized, multidisciplinary, equity-focused frameworks urgently needed.

### Aromatase Inhibitor–Induced Endothelial Dysfunction: Is It Reversible After Treatment?
- **Authors:** Dabour MS et al.
- **Journal:** Breast Cancer Research and Treatment, 2026
- **PMID:** 42329476
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1007/s10549-026-08007-2
- **Domain:** Query 4 — cardiovascular + postmenopausal breast cancer
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Important for postmenopausal breast cancer survivors on adjuvant AIs — highlights a largely irreversible cardiovascular risk that warrants active monitoring and co-management.

> In postmenopausal women on aromatase inhibitors, EndoPAT ratio was significantly impaired vs. pre-treatment (median 0.86 vs. 2.19) and declined progressively over time. Post-discontinuation (median 4.17 years follow-up), endothelial function only partially recovered (median 1.08) despite full estradiol restoration. IL-6 and TNF-α decreased post-discontinuation. Findings underscore need for cardiovascular monitoring during and after AI therapy.

### Plant-Based Dietary Subtypes and Chronic Disease Risk in Postmenopausal Women (WHI)
- **Authors:** Moosavi D et al.
- **Journal:** Journal of the Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics, 2026
- **PMID:** 42342116
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jand.2026.156406
- **Domain:** Query 4 — cardiovascular + postmenopausal (WHI)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Challenges the monolithic "plant-based is healthy" narrative for postmenopausal women — directly applicable to ALIN's nutrition guidance and lifestyle coaching.

> In WHI data (823 lower-meat vs. 823 higher-meat consumers), a Lower Meat; Dairy & Solid Fat cluster was associated with 2.6× higher CVD risk. A Lower Meat; Fish & Meat cluster was associated with 67% lower T2D risk. No plant-based subtype showed significant cancer risk reduction. Findings highlight meaningful heterogeneity within plant-based diets and caution against treating them as uniformly protective.
