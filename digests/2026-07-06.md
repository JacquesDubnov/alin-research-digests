### The Effects of Menopausal Hormone Therapy on Cardiovascular Disease, Cancer, Cognition and Depression in Younger Women: A Systematic Review
- **Authors:** Bencivenga et al.
- **Journal:** JRSM Open, 2026
- **PMID:** 42382186
- **Domain:** Query 1 (HRT/menopause) · Query 4 (cardiovascular/cancer/cognitive)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** This systematic review directly challenges one of MHT's strongest rationales — that early initiation protects the heart and brain — finding no RCT evidence for benefit across CVD, cancer, depression, or cognition. ALIN should flag this as an important counterweight to observational timing-hypothesis claims when counselling users on MHT expectations.

> Review of 31 papers from 17 RCTs found that MHT in women aged ≤60 conferred no confirmed benefit on cardiovascular disease, cancer, depression, or cognition. The partial CVD signal for estrogen-alone (WHI) was not replicated in other RCTs or in symptomatic women. Authors conclude the "timing hypothesis" for MHT benefit is not supported by randomised evidence.

### Menopausal Hormone Therapy: A Narrative Review of Contemporary Evidence
- **Authors:** Finks et al.
- **Journal:** Pharmacotherapy, 2026
- **PMID:** 42304170
- **Domain:** Query 1 (HRT/menopause)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** A current, comprehensive synthesis of MHT pharmacotherapy that ALIN can draw on to keep guidance current — covering transdermal-vs-oral risk differences, micronized progesterone preference, and low-dose vaginal estrogen safety in cancer survivors.

> Narrative review critiques WHI misinterpretation and synthesises contemporary MHT evidence: transdermal estradiol favoured for lower VTE risk; oral micronized progesterone preferred over synthetic progestogens. Evidence supports potential cardiovascular and cognitive benefit when initiated within 10 years of menopause; low-dose vaginal estrogen appears safe even in cancer survivors.

### Long-Term Oncological Implications of Hormone Replacement Therapy in Women With Hypogonadotropic Hypogonadism: A Propensity-Matched Study
- **Authors:** Eidbo et al.
- **Journal:** Cureus, 2026
- **PMID:** 42375479
- **Domain:** Query 1 (HRT/menopause)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Provides reassurance on HRT safety in a high-risk population: in 8,293 propensity-matched women with HH, HRT did not increase estrogen-associated cancer or VTE risk. Useful for ALIN users with complex hormonal histories considering HRT.

### Use of Hormone Therapy in Patients With Premature Ovarian Insufficiency in Tertiary Hospitals in Saudi Arabia
- **Authors:** Malick et al.
- **Journal:** Menopause, 2026
- **PMID:** 41556851
- **Domain:** Query 1 (HRT/menopause)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Highlights a global HT underuse crisis in POI — only 35.6% of women received it — relevant to ALIN's focus on health equity and supporting younger women with POI who most need hormonal replacement.

> Retrospective review of 205 Saudi women with POI: only 35.6% received HT despite diagnosed osteopenia/osteoporosis in the cohort. Systemic barriers, absent national guidelines, and insufficient physician training identified as primary obstacles.

- **Authors:** Sandforth et al.

- **Domain:** Query 4 (cognitive)
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Visceral fat specifically reduces hippocampal insulin sensitivity in ageing women — not men — with postmenopausal women most affected. Supports ALIN's message linking metabolic health management to cognitive protection in the menopausal transition.
