### Optimizing Estradiol Serum Levels for Skeletal and Cardiovascular Health During Transdermal MHT
- **Authors:** Piette PCM et al.
- **Journal:** Gynecological Endocrinology, 2026
- **PMID:** 42417704
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1080/09513590.2026.2694742
- **Domain:** Query 1 — HRT/MHT
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Directly supports ALIN guidance on transdermal E2 dosing targets; bridges efficacy data (VMS relief) with preventive benefits (bone, cardiovascular) at a serum E2 of ~60 pg/mL.

> Transdermal E2 gel (2.5 g/day) reduced moderate-to-severe hot flash frequency by 82%, achieving serum E2 levels within the premenopausal mid-follicular range (~60 pg/mL) — consistent with the cardiovascular and skeletal protection thresholds observed in the KEEPS and ELITE trials.

### Symptom Profiles in the Late Reproductive Stage vs. Early Perimenopause Without Vasomotor Symptoms
- **Authors:** Bresolin S et al.
- **Journal:** BMJ Sexual & Reproductive Health, 2026
- **PMID:** 41813093
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1136/bmjsrh-2025-203075
- **Domain:** Query 2 — Perimenopause
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Key evidence that changed menstrual flow alone is not a reliable diagnostic marker of perimenopause onset — relevant for ALIN's early identification features and user education around the perimenopause transition.

> In 1,039 women without vasomotor symptoms, the late reproductive stage (changed menstrual flow, regular cycles) differed minimally from early perimenopause on 18 of 22 MENQOL symptoms, suggesting changed flow alone does not reliably distinguish menopause transition onset.

### Age at Natural and Surgical Menopause and Related Factors: NHANES 2013–2023
- **Authors:** Schulz RN et al.
- **Journal:** Menopause, 2026
- **PMID:** 42413018
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1097/GME.0000000000002848
- **Domain:** Query 4 — Cardiovascular/cognitive/epidemiology
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Contemporary US epidemiological baseline for menopause timing; identifies modifiable risk factors (smoking, depression, low education) for premature menopause that ALIN can surface in personalized risk communication.

> Median age of natural menopause was 50 years (weighted mean 49.5) in 15,322 US women. Premature natural menopause prevalence: 4.6%; early menopause: 10.0%. Smoking, mild depression, and lower education were associated with earlier onset; obesity and hypertension predicted earlier surgical menopause.

### Global Prevalence of Premature or Early Menopause: 716,648 Women in 44 LMICs
- **Authors:** Islam RB et al.
- **Journal:** BMJ Global Health, 2026
- **PMID:** 42414208
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1136/bmjgh-2026-023742
- **Domain:** Query 4 — Epidemiology
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Establishes that 1 in 14 women aged 30–49 in LMICs experience premature or early menopause, with marked rural and socioeconomic gradients — important context for ALIN's global health equity framing.

> Pooled prevalence of premature or early menopause in 44 low- and middle-income countries was 7.1%, ranging from 2.3% (Jordan) to 12% (Ethiopia). Rural residence, lower education, and earlier marriage were associated with higher risk; higher education and delayed childbearing were protective.

### Early Subclinical Left Ventricular Dysfunction After Surgical Menopause
- **Authors:** Yazıcı D et al.
- **Journal:** BMC Women's Health, 2026
- **PMID:** 42426737
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1186/s12905-026-04672-z
- **Domain:** Query 4 — Cardiovascular
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Demonstrates measurable early cardiac impact of surgical menopause using sensitive imaging (speckle-tracking), reinforcing the urgency of cardiovascular monitoring and HRT consideration in women undergoing bilateral oophorectomy.

> Surgical menopause was associated with significantly worse left ventricular global longitudinal strain at 1 month post-oophorectomy vs. natural menopause (-16.9% vs. -17.6%, p=0.030), despite preserved ejection fraction, suggesting subclinical systolic dysfunction in the immediate post-surgical period.

### Regeneration of Estrogen-Deficient Vaginal Epithelium by a Novel Phytoestrogen-Related Compound
- **Authors:** Orlicky DJ et al.
- **Journal:** Menopause, 2026
- **PMID:** 42412598
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1097/GME.0000000000002820
- **Domain:** Query 1 — HRT/non-hormonal alternatives for GSM
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Preclinical proof-of-concept for a locally acting estrogenic flavone (3F) that restores vaginal epithelium without systemic estrogen activity — potentially relevant as a future non-hormonal GSM option for women who cannot use estrogen.

> In ovariectomized rats, vaginal suppository administration of 3-fluoro 6,4'-dihydroxyflavone (3F) regenerated atrophic vaginal epithelium, restored mucin secretion and antimicrobial protein expression, and showed greater mucin effect than estradiol — without stimulating vaginal or endometrial cell proliferation.

- **PMID:** 42423030
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1080/13697137.2026.2685623
- **Domain:** Query 2 — Perimenopause symptoms
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Large-scale Asian cohort data showing musculoskeletal and urogenital symptoms are most severe, with perimenopause as the peak burden period — informs ALIN's symptom tracking and diversity-aware content for Asian users.
