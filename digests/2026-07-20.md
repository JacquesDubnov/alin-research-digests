### A biopsychosocial framework for scalable menopause care: integrating cognitive behavioral therapy and digital therapeutics
- **Authors:** Grannell et al.
- **Journal:** Climacteric, 2026
- **PMID:** 42439607
- **DOI:** 10.1080/13697137.2026.2688265
- **Domain:** Query 1 — HRT/MHT & menopause
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Directly relevant — proposes integrating CBT and digital therapeutics within a biopsychosocial model for menopause care at scale, which maps closely onto ALIN's core design philosophy.

> "Menopause is a natural life stage characterized by biological, psychological and social changes that collectively influence women's health and quality of life. Despite the prevalence of vasomotor disturbances, sleep disruption, mood changes and genitourinary complaints, access to evidence-based menopause care remains limited by inadequate models of care, polarized social narratives and limited awareness among healthcare providers. The biopsychosocial model offers a holistic framework for understanding menopause, emphasizing that symptom burden is shaped not only by hormonal changes but also by individual health beliefs, coping strategies, lifestyle and social context."

### Exploring prevalence and drivers of perimenopause uncertainty among US Women: a mixed-methods study
- **Authors:** Xu et al.
- **Journal:** Menopause, 2026
- **PMID:** 42445976
- **DOI:** 10.1097/GME.0000000000002837
- **Domain:** Query 2 — perimenopause symptoms & treatment
- **Relevance to ALIN:** 34% of US women surveyed (N=7,640) were uncertain about their reproductive stage — a foundational finding for ALIN's onboarding and triage design. Uncertainty was higher in women with more severe symptoms.

> "Perimenopause is an under-recognized life stage that is often accompanied by complex and fluctuating symptoms. Overall, 34% (95% CI: 33%–35%) of participants reported being unsure of their reproductive stage. Uncertainty varied by age and symptom severity (P < 0.001)."

### Lipoprotein(a) and incident venous thromboembolism in pre- and postmenopausal women, and in men
- **Authors:** Ezzat et al.
- **Journal:** European Heart Journal, 2026
- **PMID:** 41903523
- **DOI:** 10.1093/eurheartj/ehag252
- **Domain:** Query 4 — cardiovascular / VTE / thromboembolism
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Elevated Lp(a) ≥125 nmol/L as a VTE risk factor with sex- and menopausal status–stratified analysis from UK Biobank — adds nuance to risk counselling around HRT and thrombosis.

> "Elevated lipoprotein(a) [Lp(a)] levels are an established risk factor for atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease, but the association between Lp(a) and venous thromboembolism (VTE) remains unclear. Sex and hormonal status may modify the relationship between Lp(a) and VTE."

### Women's Treatment Preferences for Moderate to Severe Vasomotor Symptoms Associated with Menopause: Insights from the WARMER Study
- **Authors:** Heidenreich et al.
- **Journal:** The Patient, 2026
- **PMID:** 42458154
- **DOI:** 10.1007/s40271-026-00831-4
- **Domain:** Query 3 — non-hormonal / fezolinetant / vasomotor symptoms
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Discrete choice experiment across 8 countries (Australia, Canada, Denmark, France, Germany, Spain, Sweden, UK) quantifying how women trade off efficacy vs. long-term safety concerns for VMS treatments — informs how ALIN should frame treatment options.

> "Vasomotor symptoms (VMS) affect up to 80% of women over the course of their menopause and can impair functioning as well as quality of life. Hormone therapy (HT) is the standard of care, but concerns about long-term safety may be a barrier to uptake. Emerging non-hormonal therapies could be an alternative for some women."

### Overlap Between Heart Failure and Menopause: Diagnostic Challenges and Clinical Implications
- **Authors:** Kwan et al.
- **Journal:** Heart, Lung & Circulation, 2026
- **PMID:** 42463399
- **DOI:** 10.1016/j.hlc.2026.05.011
- **Domain:** Query 4 — cardiovascular / menopause
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Oestrogen's cardioprotective role and its decline as a driver of myocardial fibrosis and diastolic dysfunction — important for ALIN users whose cardiac symptoms overlap with classic menopausal complaints.

> "Heart failure and menopause commonly coexist in middle-aged women, but overlapping symptomatology often complicates diagnosis. Oestrogen exerts cardioprotective effects through anti-inflammatory and vasodilatory pathways and its decline has been proposed as a mechanistic explanation for myocardial fibrosis and diastolic dysfunction."

- **PMID:** 42470110
- **DOI:** 10.1210/clinem/dgag271
- **Domain:** Query 4 — breast cancer / menopause
- **Relevance to ALIN:** Register-based Finnish cohort (n=31,688 pre-eclamptic women vs 91,726 controls, follow-up 1987–2023) showing pre-eclampsia history modifies the MHT–breast cancer risk relationship — supports personalized risk assessment in ALIN.
